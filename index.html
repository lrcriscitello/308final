The Perfect Pumpkin Tiramisu

A delicious dessert for coffee lovers and fall fanatics everywhere!

About the Recipe

About the Recipe

One day, I was thinking to myself, "I wish there was a way to combine two of my favorite desserts, Pumpkin Pie and Tiramisu", but I quickly found out, there is. This dessert is perfect for any coffee lover or fall fanatic who wants to bring a little spice to the dessert table this year. This fluffy, creamy Tiramisu will have family and friends asking, "did you really make this?". While making this dessert, I noted that there were many benefits to making this recipe:

  • Few ingredient steps
  • No-Bake Dessert - only requires cooling
  • Easy cleanup

Info and Ingredients

Recipe Information
  • Level: Easy
  • Active Time: 50 min
  • Total Time: 5 hr 10 min (Includes Chilling)
  • Yield: 8 to 10 servings
Ingredients
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup bourbon
  • 1/2 pound mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • One 15-ounce can pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup loosely packed brown sugar
  • 3 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • One large bottle of cold brew coffee
  • 45 store-bought ladyfingers
  • 36 crushed gingersnap cookies (1 1/2 cups total)
  • Semi-sweet chocolate bar (for grating)
  • Cinnamon
  • Nutmeg

Step-by-Step Directions

Step 1: Making the Custard

1. To make the custard: Bring some water to a boil in a saucepan, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Find a heatproof mixing bowl that will fit over the top of the pan, but won't fall in. (DIY double boiler!)

2. Next, place the egg yolks in the mixing bowl. Add the granulated sugar and whisk until the yolks start to turn pale yellow. Place the mixing bowl on top of the saucepan with the simmering water. Slowly add the bourbon and whisk to combine. Cook over the simmering water, making sure to scrape the sides of the bowl, until thick, for about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until cool, for 20 to 25 minutes.

Step 2: Making the Pumpkin Mixture

3. Place the mascarpone cheese and pumpkin puree in a bowl and stir/mix until smooth. Combine the whipping cream, brown sugar, vanilla and cinnamon in a separate mixing bowl and whip until not quite stiff. Add the mascarpone and pumpkin mixture to the whipped cream mixture, followed by the chilled egg mixture. Fold gently until mixed well. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.

Step 3: Assembling the Tiramisu

4. To assemble: Arrange the ladyfingers in a single layer in a 9-by-13-inch pan, breaking as necessary to fit. Dunk each ladyfinger in the cold brew coffee before placing it in the pan. Plop a third of the chilled mascarpone/pumpkin mixture on top and spread it into a thin layer. Grate over a thin layer of chocolate and sprinkle over 1/2 cup of the crushed gingersnaps.

5. Repeat the process 2 more times, finishing with the crushed gingersnaps. Cover and refrigerate for 3 hours or up to overnight before serving. This will allow for more moisture to soften the cookies and for the whole mixture to meld together. The tiramisu is best consumed within 24 to 36 hours after assembling, as the components will begin to break down and become soupy.

Behind the Tiramisu

This Thanksgiving, I wanted to make a dessert that would challenge me and exceed my family's expections. I can say confidently that this Pumpkin Tiramisu did exactly that. Since it is a "no-bake" dessert, it gave me plenty of time to focus on the other dishes I was making as well. When I brought out this treat after a long thankgiving dinner, my family was absolutely ectstatic. We have been a coffee-loving household for some time, so this was the dish for us. The bourbon integrated beautifully even though none of it bakes out (and let me tell you there's a lot of bourbon in there). It had a beautifully boozey finish and was a must-have at this years dinner.

As someone who regularly bakes pies, cakes, and cookies, I had limited baking experience going into this adventure. I had never made a custard or anything of the such and was a little apprehensive when I started. After making this dish, I can firmly say that ANYONE can make it. Although many of the steps look daunting or complex, the recipe itself is really quite simple. I HIGHLY recommend this recipe to anyone who wants to advance their skills in the kitchen.
